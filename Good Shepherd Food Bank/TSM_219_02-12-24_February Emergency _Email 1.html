Recent data shows that Maine now has the highest rate of food insecurity in New England, with nearly 145,000 people living in food-insecure households and 1 in 7 children facing hunger. The loss of $17 million in supplemental food benefits every month, combined with higher food costs, means more families are turning to Good Shepherd Food Bank and local pantries for help.

We urgently need your help to continue meeting this increased need and ensure Maine families have enough food.

Additionally, many Maine food pantries are reporting that the need is higher than at the start of the pandemic.

The big challenge is that donations are down as need is skyrocketing. We must expand our services and reach so that no one has to go without the food they need.

Good Shepherd Food Bank and the local organizations we partner with must step up to serve our communities as Maine households face this increased hardship. We can’t do it without your help.

Your gift is urgently needed. Please give today. Every $10 you give can help provide 20 meals for our neighbors, which makes such a big difference.
