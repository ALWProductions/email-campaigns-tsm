How You’re Helping Seniors This Spring
 
Dear *|FNAME|*,

We’re getting older. Not just you and me and Good Shepherd Food Bank, but the entire state of Maine, which has the highest concentration of seniors in the U.S.

Our average age is 45 years, which places our state as one of the oldest in the nation. And our senior population is growing rapidly — often with families on fixed incomes that just don’t stretch far enough. Once our neighbors pay their monthly bills, there’s often barely enough left for food.

One in six seniors in Maine is at risk of hunger. That’s too high, but that’s also why I’m grateful for friends like you who can make such a difference for our older neighbors. You’ll read about some of them in our online edition of Helping Hands, where you’ll also meet some of our most dedicated volunteers.

On behalf of all the Mainers we serve, thank you!

Kristen Miale
President
