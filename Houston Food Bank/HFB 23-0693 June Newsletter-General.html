Dear Friend,

In the midst of high cost of food, families facing hunger are impacted twice as much this summer.

First, there’s the loss of free or reduced-price meals that thousands of children typically get in school. Second, a recent reduction in SNAP benefits is devastating for many of our neighbors’ budgets.

As you’ll read below, some families are seeing cuts of more than $400 per month. Can you imagine losing $400 that you count on for groceries each month?

Thank you for your support, especially during these challenging summer months. I hope you will take a moment to read about the difference you are making in your neighbors’ lives and other important things going on at the Food Bank.

Sincerely,

Brian Greene
President & CEO
“We Really Need It”
Not so long ago, Jennifer’s typical grocery bill was about $130 to support her family of four — including her 7-year-old son Jose. But in the summer, Jose no longer has access to free or reduced-price meals at school. And even when cutting back at the store, Jennifer can hardly believe the cash register as it tallies her total. That’s why this mom is so grateful for people like you.

Earlier this year, we received a hydroponic farming container from Freight Farms, an agricultural tech company. Every week new seeds are planted and we are growing radishes, romaine, lettuce, and more. To give you an idea of the potential size of a single harvest, this new tech could help us grow 3,900 heads of lettuce!

Volunteering can be hard work. But it’s also rewarding.
Just ask Destiny, a volunteer at one of the Food Bank’s community partners. She helps visitors get the food, water, and essentials they need. She’s been in their shoes, too.

As a student, Destiny appreciated receiving backpacks filled with school supplies. She recalls, “It really benefited me.” And now, she embraces the opportunity to give back.
